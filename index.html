Blood Donation Rules — Quick Reference
Who can donate, who cannot, and when to wait
📋
✅ CAN DONATE
  • Age 18 – 65 years
  • Weight at least 45 kg
  • In good health
  • Last donation was 3+ months ago (Whole blood)
  • Well-rested and had a meal before donating
  • Hemoglobin ≥ 12.5 g/dL (Women), ≥ 13 g/dL (Men)
❌ CANNOT DONATE
  • Age below 18 or above 65
  • Weight below 45 kg
  • Insulin-dependent Diabetics
  • HIV, Hepatitis B/C positive
  • Pregnant women or breastfeeding mothers
  • On Blood thinners (Warfarin, Aspirin)
  • Heart disease or Cancer patients
  • Within 24 hours of consuming alcohol
⏳ WAIT PERIOD
  • Fever / Cold — 7 days after recovery
  • Tattoo / Piercing — 6 months
  • Surgery — 6 months
  • Malaria — 3 months after recovery
  • Typhoid — 1 year after recovery
  • Antibiotics — 7 days after stopping
  • Delivery / Miscarriage — 6 months
  • Between Whole Blood donations — 3 months (90 days)
💡 Donation Types — How often can you donate?
  • Whole Blood — 90 days (3 months)
  • Platelets — 7 days (max 24 times/year)
  • Plasma — 28 days
  • Double Red Cells — 112 days
